# Personal AI Infrastructure

> AI changes. Your digital life shouldn't.

This repository is a public notebook for exploring one question:

**Can a person's digital life exist independently of any AI company?**

## Core Idea

AI models will keep changing.

Today it may be ChatGPT.  
Tomorrow it may be Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, or something completely new.

But personal data, memory, knowledge, files, workflows, and digital history should not be locked inside any single AI product.

The goal is to explore a personal AI infrastructure where:

- digital assets belong to the user;
- memory is managed by the user;
- AI models only access the context they need;
- models can be replaced without losing personal history;
- automation, tools, and knowledge can evolve over time.

## Current Questions

- Should AI own memory, or should it only access user-owned memory?
- How can personal digital assets be collected and organized?
- How should memory be indexed, summarized, tagged, and updated?
- How can privacy and permission control be built into the system?
- What should a self-hosted personal AI infrastructure look like?

## Status

This is an early-stage research and engineering log.

No final answer yet.

I will document ideas, mistakes, design decisions, and prototypes here.

## Philosophy

> Own your digital life. Let any AI work for you.

## Principles

1. Ownership belongs to the user.
2. AI models are replaceable.
3. Memory is independent of models.
4. Infrastructure should outlive AI products.
5. Privacy comes before convenience.
