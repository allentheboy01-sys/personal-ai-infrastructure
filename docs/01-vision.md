# Vision

## Why PDI Exists

I did not start this project because I wanted to build another AI assistant.

I started it because I realized that every AI system builds its own memory.

Today, ChatGPT has its memory.

Claude has its memory.

Gemini has its memory.

Every new AI product asks people to start over.

The memory belongs to the AI.

BUT Not to the person.

I believe this is the wrong direction.

AI systems will continue to evolve.

People should not have to rebuild their digital lives every time they change AI.

PDI begins with a simple belief.

> **A person's digital life should belong to the person, not to any AI product.**

---

## What Changed My Thinking

When I first started this project, I wanted to build an AI that could truly understand me.

As I continued exploring AI systems, I realized that intelligence was not the biggest limitation.

The real limitation was the lack of a persistent digital foundation.

Without a shared representation of a person's digital life, every AI can only understand a small part of that person.

So I started asking,

> **"What should exist before AI?"**

That question eventually became PDI.

---

## What PDI Is

PDI is not another AI assistant.

PDI is not another large language model.

PDI is not another cloud storage platform.

PDI is a long-term digital infrastructure designed for people.

Its purpose is to organize a person's digital life so that it remains understandable across generations of AI systems while always belonging to the individual.

AI becomes one of its users.

Not its owner.

---

## Long-term Goal

The purpose of PDI is not to make AI more powerful.

It is to make AI more useful to people.

I hope one day AI can genuinely help people throughout everyday life.

Not simply by answering questions.

But by understanding context.

Remembering what matters.

Helping people make better decisions.

Reducing unnecessary effort.

Supporting people over years rather than conversations.

I believe AI has the potential to improve human life.

PDI is my attempt to build the foundation that allows it to do so responsibly.

---

## Core Beliefs

Everything in PDI is guided by a small number of principles.

- AI systems are replaceable.
- Personal digital life is persistent.
- Digital memory belongs to people.
- Infrastructure should outlive individual technologies.
- Research comes before implementation.
- Engineering should answer research questions.
- Every important assumption should remain open to discussion.

---

## Origins

PDI did not begin as an infrastructure project.

It began with a much simpler idea.

I wanted to build an AI assistant that could truly understand me.

During my first year at university, I became deeply interested in artificial intelligence. As I explored modern AI systems and experimented with different AI agents, I kept encountering the same limitation.

Every AI could answer my questions.

None of them could truly know me.

At first, I believed the solution was to build my own AI agent.

The more I explored, however, the more I realized that an agent was only one possible interface.

It was not the real problem.

The real problem was that every AI system builds its own memory.

Every application stores a different version of the same person.

Changing AI often means rebuilding context from the beginning.

That realization completely changed the direction of this project.

Instead of building another AI assistant, I started exploring the infrastructure that should exist before AI.

Throughout this journey, many of my original ideas proved to be wrong.

Architectures were redesigned.

Assumptions were challenged.

Concepts were repeatedly rebuilt.

Rather than reinventing every technology, I gradually learned to build upon existing systems and focus on the problems that remain unsolved.

PDI is the result of that ongoing exploration.

It is not the conclusion of my research.

It is where my research begins.
