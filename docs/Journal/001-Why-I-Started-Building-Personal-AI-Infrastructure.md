# 2026-06-27

## Why did I start thinking about this?

I've always wanted something similar to **Jarvis**.

Not simply another chatbot, but a long-term personal digital companion that can continuously organize my digital life, automate repetitive work, help me make decisions, and evolve together with me over decades.

However, today's AI products all share one limitation.

Each AI owns its own memory.

Each application owns its own context.

Every time I switch from one AI model to another, I almost have to start over.

This feels fundamentally wrong.

---

## Current Observation

Large language models are evolving extremely fast.

Today's best model may no longer be the best one next year.

However, my digital life should not depend on which AI company I happen to use.

My knowledge, projects, files, conversations, automation workflows, and personal context should belong to **me**, not to any AI provider.

AI should simply become an intelligent engine that temporarily accesses my information when necessary.

---

## Today's Biggest Realization

Initially, I believed this project was about building a better AI assistant.

Now I think that's not the real goal.

The real goal is to build **Jarvis**.

Jarvis is **not** an AI model.

Jarvis is a long-term personal digital infrastructure.

AI models are only interchangeable intelligence engines that Jarvis can temporarily use.

The relationship should look like this:

Harry

↓

Jarvis

↓

GPT / Claude / Gemini / DeepSeek / Future Models

instead of

Harry

↓

One AI Company

---

## Information Loss

One problem I noticed today is information loss.

For example:

I ask one AI to summarize everything about me.

Then I copy that summary into another AI.

Every summarization compresses information.

Every compression loses details.

Eventually, the new AI receives only an approximation of my real history.

Instead, AI should access original user-owned context whenever possible, rather than repeatedly consuming summaries generated by other AI models.

---

## New Direction

I no longer believe Memory is the core problem.

Memory is only one component.

The larger challenge is building a long-term digital infrastructure that can survive multiple generations of AI models.

This infrastructure should:

- Continuously collect digital assets.
- Organize them automatically.
- Protect privacy through permission control.
- Continuously evolve over time.
- Remain independent from any specific AI model.
